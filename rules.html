Rules

Breaking the rules will result in disqualification of you and your team. Please read them carefully and make sure you understand. If you have any questions, feel free to ask.

Team Guidelines:

  • Each team can have a maximum of three members. Individual participation is also welcome. example 1 for explanation
  • Each participant can be a member of only one team.
  • Teams can be formed before or during the hackathon.
  • Changing teams is not allowed once registered.
  • Although not necessary, it is highly recommended to be a part of the Discord Community of Lewis Menelaws as all of our communication will be conducted there first.

Project Guidelines:

  • All code, design, and assets must be created during the hackathon.
  • Utilization of third-party libraries and services is allowed as long as they are freely available to other participants and do not require any form of payment (ex: card).
  • You are free to use any technology to work on your project.
  • Your project must align with the theme of the hackathon.
  • You own all the code you write, but by submitting a project, you allow us to showcase and promote it.
  • Web applications are encouraged, although all kinds of entries are welcome.

Submission Guidelines:

  • All your work must be open-sourced on GitHub.
  • You must include presentable screenshots of your project along with an optional (but recommended) demo video.
  • Submit your project by the deadline; any submissions made after the deadline will NOT be evaluated. Only the latest submission will be considered.

We reserve the right to update (refine) the rules at any time. If we introduce major changes, we will notify you on our Discord server.
